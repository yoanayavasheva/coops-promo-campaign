The Co-operative Alternative

POV: You are graduating in a few months, and feel daunted by the rising levels of competition and precarity in the post-graduate workforce.

Have you heard of worker co-operatives?

The International Cooperative Alliance (ICA) defines a co-operative as "an autonomous association of persons united voluntarily to meet their common economic, social and cultural needs and aspirations through a jointly-owned and democratically-controlled enterprise."

A co-operative is an alternative business ownership model that favors member welfare and sustainable production amongst other values. In a co-op the members are also the owners of the business. The key feature of one-member-one-vote creates a distinctly flat structure within between members.

The 7 Co-op Principles

  1. Voluntary and Open Membership
  2. Democratic Member Control
  3. Member Economic Participation
  4. Autonomy and Independence
  5. Education, Training, and Information
  6. Cooperation among Cooperatives
  7. Concern for Community

Benefits of working in a co-op

  • Equal and shared decision-making
  • Workplace autonomy
  • Sustainable and secure employment
  • Work guided by internal values
  • Favour of member and community welfare
  • Limited ownership liability
  • Boosted productivity
  • Elevated creativity
  • Higher levels of diversity
  • Higher likelihood of start-up survival

A happy life is more than just about maximizing profit.
But, we do need economic stability to be happy and well.

Frequently asked questions

  • Q: How do I start my own co-operative?
  • Whether you want to start or convert an existing business into a co-operative, here is a guide by Co-operatives UK that may assist you.
  • Q: How do I prepare for an interview at a co-op?
  • Prepare for a co-op interview by reflecting on how your skills and previous experience will apply to the position. Familiarize yourself with the co-op principles and think a little about why working at a co-op is desirable for you.
    Here is an article by ILAC on "Common Co-op Interview Questions" that may help guide you further in your preparation.
  • Q: Where can I find co-operatives in the UK?
  • Co-operatives UK has created a directory here that shows registered co-ops accross the UK. Search through the map and if you find a co-op you like, reach out! (Even if they aren't looking for positions!)
